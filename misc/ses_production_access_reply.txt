WHAT I SEND

HOW MY LIST IS BUILT
All recipients are genuine opt-ins. People subscribe through a signup form on my own website (andreatarroni.com), where they explicitly request to join the list. I also invite engaged members of my existing audience (from a separate, related project of mine) to opt in separately if they're interested in this specific newsletter - this is not a bulk import of any existing list, just a slow trickle of new, deliberate sign-ups. Every contact record stores consent metadata (timestamp, source, and IP address of signup) so I can demonstrate how and when consent was given.

HOW I HANDLE BOUNCES, COMPLAINTS, AND UNSUBSCRIBES
I built a small self-hosted application specifically for this purpose, with deliverability handled as a first-class concern, not an afterthought:

- Every email includes a one-click unsubscribe link in the footer, plus a List-Unsubscribe header (RFC 8058) so Gmail/Yahoo/Outlook can offer their native one-click unsubscribe. Clicking it immediately and permanently removes the contact from future sends.
- I have a webhook endpoint that receives SES bounce and complaint notifications via SNS in real time. Permanent bounces and any complaint immediately and automatically suppress that contact from all future sends - no manual intervention needed. Transient bounces are not auto-suppressed since they're often temporary (e.g. mailbox full).
- This suppression logic is already deployed and verified end-to-end against my domain identity (mail.andreatarroni.com), including a real SNS subscription handshake confirmed in the SNS console.
- I only send to contacts with an "active" status; bounced, complained, or unsubscribed contacts are automatically excluded from every send.

SENDING IDENTITY
I send exclusively from a dedicated subdomain, mail.andreatarroni.com, which is fully authenticated with SPF, DKIM, and a custom MAIL FROM domain aligned for DMARC. This isolates this newsletter's sending reputation from my main domain's regular email traffic.
